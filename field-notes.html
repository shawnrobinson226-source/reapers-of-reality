Field Notes

What Field Notes Are

Field Notes are the record of reality in contact with the work. They are not essays, confessions, or performances. They are written observations from lived moments where a pattern, choice, consequence, or correction became visible.

Their purpose is simple: turn experience into evidence. What remains vague can be explained away. What is recorded can be studied, tested, and changed.

Method

How to Write a Field Note

Write the note close to the event while the details are still clean. Start with what happened, not what it means. Name the situation, the trigger, the reaction, the choice, and the outcome.

Keep the note direct enough to be useful later. A strong field note does not inflate the moment. It preserves the signal so the pattern can be seen without decoration.

Tracking

What to Track

Track the places where your claimed values meet pressure. Notice repeated triggers, emotional spikes, avoidance moves, broken commitments, clean corrections, and moments where the new standard held.

Also track the conditions around the event: time, fatigue, environment, people involved, and what you were protecting. Patterns rarely appear alone. They travel with context.

Evidence

Pattern Evidence

Pattern evidence is the repeated proof that something is not random. One reaction may be a moment. Three similar reactions across different situations may be a structure.

Use Field Notes to gather that proof without exaggeration. Record what repeats, what it costs, what it protects, and what it keeps producing. Evidence gives the work its edge.

Signals

Drift Signals

Drift begins before collapse. It shows up as softer language, skipped commitments, delayed decisions, hidden resentment, quiet bargaining, or the return of habits that were already named.

A drift signal is not a verdict. It is an early warning. Write it down while it is still small enough to correct without drama.

Correction

Correction Notes

Correction Notes turn observation into the next clean act. They name what needs to change without turning the mistake into an identity or the lesson into a speech.

A useful correction note includes the missed standard, the cause you can honestly own, the repair if one is needed, and the next behavior that proves the lesson was received.

Review

Weekly Review

The weekly review is where scattered notes become direction. Read the week as evidence: what repeated, what improved, what slipped, and what consequence is forming.

End the review with one pattern to watch, one correction to practice, and one standard to protect. The point is not to judge the week perfectly. The point is to return with clarity.
